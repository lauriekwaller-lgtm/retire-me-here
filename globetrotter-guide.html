Before You Scroll

Four things to know about airport access in retirement.

1
Florida and Texas dominate the top. Tampa, Fort Worth, and Miami anchor the elite tier with world-class hubs, and Delray Beach's three-airport access (PBI, FLL, MIA) is unmatched anywhere in the database. Warm weather plus deep connectivity is a rare combination, and the Sun Belt has it.
2
The mountain trade-off is the sharpest in the database. Park City (SLC 35 min) and Jackson Hole (remarkable nonstops for its size) score well, but Sedona, Durango, Flagstaff, and Prescott all sit at 3. If you're drawn to the mountain Southwest and travel often, this is the gap that matters most.
3
A few small cities punch far above their weight. Nashua reaches both Manchester and Boston Logan; Jackson Hole has remarkable nonstop service for a remote valley; Fayetteville rides corporate demand from Walmart and Tyson. Size doesn't always predict connectivity.
4
Not all airports are equal — and "has an airport" isn't the question. A true hub like DFW, TPA, or LAS offers hundreds of nonstops; a regional field like Asheville or Chattanooga leaves you one connection from everywhere, usually through Atlanta or Charlotte. Over years of travel, that difference compounds.

Elite Access — Scores 9–10

Major hubs within reach, with outstanding national and international connectivity. These are the cities where getting almost anywhere means a single nonstop flight.

DFW minutes away — American's global hub, nonstop to 250+ destinations on 6 continents. Among the best airport access of any city in the database.

TampaFL 10/10
TPA — major Delta, American, Southwest, United hub. Compact and easy, 90+ nonstop destinations including international. Routinely rated among the best US airports for travelers.
→
MiamiFL 10/10
MIA — one of the great international gateways, especially for Latin America and the Caribbean, plus deep European service. Few US airports match its global reach.
→
Salt Lake CityUT 10/10
SLC at the city's edge — major Delta hub, 98+ nonstop destinations including Europe and Asia. Rare major-metro connectivity with wilderness fifteen minutes away.

St. PaulMN 10/10
MSP 20 min — major Delta, American, Southwest hub with nonstop Europe service. One of the 15 busiest US airports by volume.

DTW 30 min — major Delta hub, Delta's second-largest, with nonstop Europe, Asia, and Caribbean service. Outstanding connectivity for a college town.
