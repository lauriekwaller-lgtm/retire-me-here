How we scored airport access

Each city was evaluated on three factors: drive time to the nearest commercial airport, the quality and frequency of that airport's connections (number of airlines, nonstop destinations, hub status), and backup airport options within a reasonable distance.

A score of 10 means you're minutes from a major international hub with dozens of nonstop destinations. A score of 3 means your nearest airport has limited routes and you'll likely need a 2+ hour drive to reach meaningful connections. The tradeoffs are real — some of the most beautiful retirement cities in the country are also the most isolated.

Key Takeaways — Before You Scroll

✈️ The Florida Advantage

Florida dominates the top tier — Tampa, Fort Myers, Delray Beach, and Naples all score 8 or above. The combination of warm winters and exceptional airport access makes Florida uniquely compelling for retirees who travel frequently. Delray Beach's three-airport access (PBI, FLL, MIA) is unmatched by any other city in the database.

🏔️ The Mountain Trade-Off

The most dramatic airport gap in the database is between mountain cities. Park City (SLC 35 min, score 8) and Bozeman (BZN on-site, score 7) offer surprisingly strong connectivity. But Sedona, Durango, Flagstaff, and Prescott all score 3 — a world away. If you're drawn to the mountain Southwest and travel frequently, this is the tradeoff that matters most.

🌟 Hidden Gems for Travelers

Three cities offer airport access that surprises relative to their size: Nashua NH gets you to both Manchester (20 min) and Boston Logan (45 min) — world-class international access from a quiet New England city. Jackson Hole WY has remarkable nonstop service given its remote location. Fayetteville AR punches above its weight due to corporate demand from Walmart and Tyson Foods headquarters.

🚇 Alexandria: The Only City Where Metro Takes You to the Gate

Alexandria is the only city in the database where you can walk from your front door to the Metro, ride to Reagan National Airport, and be at your gate — all without a car, rideshare, or parking fee. The Blue and Yellow lines stop directly at the terminal. For retirees who travel frequently and are thinking about aging-in-place without driving, this combination of walkable neighborhoods and transit-to-airport access is genuinely unique among all 59 cities we score.

📍 What "Hub" Really Means

Not all airports are equal. Fort Worth's DFW, Tampa's TPA, and Henderson's LAS are world-class hubs with nonstop service to hundreds of destinations including international. A city like Asheville or Chattanooga has an airport, but you're one connection away from everywhere — and that connection is Atlanta or Charlotte, adding 2+ hours to every international trip. The difference matters over years of travel.
