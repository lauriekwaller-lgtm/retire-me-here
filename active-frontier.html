Two dimensions, one active retirement

We score each city on two separate dimensions: Outdoor Recreation (D7) evaluates natural assets — proximity to national parks, mountains, beaches, rivers, trails, and the quality of the outdoor experience available. Active Wellness (D8) evaluates the built fitness infrastructure — recreation centers, pickleball courts, fitness classes, senior programming, spa culture, and the overall community commitment to active aging.

The best cities score high on both. But the tradeoffs are real — some mountain cities offer world-class outdoor recreation but limited indoor wellness infrastructure. Some resort communities have exceptional wellness amenities but limited true wilderness access. Understanding both scores together tells the full picture.

Key Takeaways — Before You Scroll

🏆 The Perfect Balance: Scottsdale and Naples

Only two cities in the database score 10/10 on Active Wellness: Scottsdale and Naples. Scottsdale is the undisputed pickleball capital of the US with 200+ golf courses, world-class resort spas, and the McDowell Sonoran Preserve for hiking. Naples has the most complete 55+ active adult infrastructure of any Florida city — and NCH Healthcare to back it up. Both also score 8–9 on outdoor recreation. They represent the gold standard of active retirement living.

🏔️ The Mountain Outdoor Crown

Four cities share a D7 score of 10 — the highest outdoor recreation rating: St. George UT, Park City UT, Jackson Hole WY, and Bend OR. Each offers a fundamentally different outdoor experience. St. George has Zion and Snow Canyon. Park City has two world-class ski resorts and 450+ miles of trails. Jackson Hole has Grand Teton as a literal backyard. Bend has Mt. Bachelor and the Cascade range. If outdoor recreation is your primary retirement driver, these four cities are in a class of their own.

🌴 Florida's Active Adult Infrastructure

Florida has invested heavily in active adult community infrastructure and it shows in the D8 scores. Henderson NV (Sun City Anthem), Georgetown TX (Sun City Texas), Naples, Scottsdale, Delray Beach, Fort Myers, and Palm Springs all score 8–10 on wellness — not because of their wilderness, but because their active adult communities and resort amenities are purpose-built for exactly this lifestyle. If structured fitness and community programming matter more than wilderness access, Florida and the Sun Belt deliver.

⚡ The Surprising Overachievers

Greenville SC, Chattanooga TN, Fayetteville AR, and Missoula MT all punch above their weight on outdoor recreation. Greenville's Swamp Rabbit Trail and Blue Ridge access at this price point is remarkable. Chattanooga's Lookout Mountain and Tennessee River activities are genuinely impressive. Fayetteville's Ozark trails and Buffalo National River are world-class but rarely mentioned in national rankings. Missoula has three rivers converging in the city and wilderness beginning at the edge of town.

❄️ The Winter Question

Several high-scoring outdoor cities have a seasonal asterisk. St. Paul scores well for its trail system and lakes — but outdoor activity is genuinely limited November through March. Bozeman and Jackson Hole trade summer hiking for world-class skiing. Asheville's Blue Ridge Parkway is spectacular in fall but winter hiking is limited. When evaluating outdoor recreation scores, always ask: is this a year-round asset or a seasonal one?
