The ActiveFrontier

For retirees who want their lifestyle defined by being outside — mountain or coast, summer or winter, hiking or pickleball. This guide ranks our database of 100 cities by the depth and year-round usability of their outdoor recreation.

🏔 100 Cities Scored
🥾 Trails · Water · Snow
🗓 May 2026
What we looked for: substantive outdoor recreation infrastructure (trails, parks, water access, ski terrain), real activity depth (hiking, cycling, water sports, golf and pickleball at scale), and year-round usability. Cities with strong indoor fitness scenes but thin outdoor recreation are covered in our companion report, the Wellness Blueprint, and on our Top Cities for Active Retirees page.

Before You Scroll

Four things to know about outdoor retirement.

1
The 10/10 crown is shared. Ten cities tie for top outdoor recreation in the database: Asheville, Bend, Boulder, Flagstaff, Jackson Hole, Park City, St. George, Salt Lake City, Sun Valley, and Vail. Each offers a fundamentally different version of "outdoor" — Blue Ridge forest, Cascade alpine, Sonoran desert, Tetons, Wasatch. The crown is wide.
2
Climate is the real divider. A 10/10 outdoor city in Montana is not the same lifestyle as a 10/10 outdoor city in Florida. Mountain towns trade summer hiking for winter skiing. Sun Belt cities trade winter mildness for brutal summers. The first question isn't "how good is the outdoor recreation" — it's "what season do you want to be outside?"
3
Outdoor club sports concentrate in four states. Pickleball, golf, and tennis at scale — purpose-built for active 55+ communities — live in Arizona, Nevada, Florida, and Texas. Scottsdale, Naples, Henderson, and Sun City Texas (Georgetown) anchor this category. The infrastructure is unmatched, but it requires the Sun Belt climate and the master-planned community model to exist at this scale.
4
College towns punch above their weight. Boulder, Bozeman, Bentonville, Fort Collins, Knoxville, Madison, Missoula, La Crosse, Bloomington — university towns benefit from athletic infrastructure, young-skewing fitness cultures, and decades of trail and recreation investment. Several appear here at price points far below resort towns offering similar outdoor access.

Spotlight 1

The Outdoor Capitals — Top 10

These ten cities score the highest combined marks in the database for outdoor recreation and active community infrastructure. They're geographically diverse — Pacific Northwest, Rockies, Southern Appalachians, Sonoran desert, Wasatch Front. Each offers a distinct version of an outdoor retirement.

1
BoulderCO
The fitness mecca of the American outdoors. 300+ trail miles, 45,000 acres of city-funded protected open space (in place since 1967), the Flatirons rising directly from town. Saturated masters athletics culture; world-class running, cycling, and climbing communities. Pearl Street pedestrian mall and 300+ days of sunshine. Expensive ($800K–$1M median) but unmatched if you can afford it.
2
BendOR
Mt. Bachelor skiing 30 minutes from downtown. Cascades hiking — South Sister, Broken Top, Crater Lake. Deschutes River fly fishing and kayaking. Smith Rock State Park world-class climbing. High Desert wilderness at the doorstep. Four-season outdoor access is genuine and extraordinary; high desert at 3,600 ft means 300 sunny days and dry summers.
3
Park CityUT
Two world-class ski resorts — Park City Mountain and Deer Valley — literally in the backyard. 450+ miles of hiking and mountain biking trails. Utah Olympic Park venues open to residents. Summer and winter equally compelling. SLC airport 35 minutes. The premium mountain resort retirement destination in the West.
4
AshevilleNC
Blue Ridge Parkway through town — one of the most scenic drives in America. Pisgah National Forest at the city edge. Appalachian Trail accessible. French Broad whitewater rafting. World-class mountain biking on Bent Creek trails. Outdoor recreation rivaling cities three times the price.
5
Jackson HoleWY
Grand Teton National Park as the literal backyard. Yellowstone an hour away. Snake River blue-ribbon trout fishing. Jackson Hole Mountain Resort — one of North America's most challenging ski mountains. 1,000+ miles of trails. The most dramatic outdoor setting in the database; no Wyoming income tax to soften the price.
6
St. GeorgeUT
Snow Canyon State Park inside city limits. Zion National Park 45 minutes, Bryce Canyon 90 minutes. Red Cliffs National Conservation Area 15 minutes. 300+ days of sunshine. One of the leading pickleball cities in the US — dozens of dedicated outdoor courts. Density of world-class outdoor assets within driving distance is unmatched.
7
BozemanMT
Bridger Bowl 20 minutes; Big Sky Resort 60 minutes — one of the largest ski resorts in North America. Yellowstone 90 minutes. Three blue-ribbon trout streams within the city. Gallatin National Forest, Hyalite Canyon. Outdoor density rivals anywhere in the Mountain West, with MSU energy year-round.
8
SedonaAZ
100+ trails including Cathedral Rock, Devil's Bridge, and West Fork Canyon. World-class mountain biking. Oak Creek swimming holes; Slide Rock State Park. Mild winters at 4,500 ft mean year-round red rock trail access. The most scenic trail system of any city in the database.
9
Salt Lake CityUT
11 ski resorts within an hour. Wasatch Front trails immediately accessible from the Avenues and East Bench. Major Delta hub at the city's edge. The rare combination of major-metro infrastructure with genuine wilderness access fifteen minutes from downtown.
10
Colorado SpringsCO
Garden of the Gods — free, world-class red rock formations within city limits. Pikes Peak summit accessible by car or cog railway. 14ers within striking distance. Cheyenne Mountain State Park. Mountain access anchored by an unusually strong military fitness culture and US Olympic and Paralympic Training Center events.
