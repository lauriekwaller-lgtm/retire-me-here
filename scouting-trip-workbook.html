The Scouting Trip Workbook

Print this and take it with you. The framework from Visit Before You Decide, structured as a worksheet you can fill in as you go and refer back to when the decision is in front of you.

Pre-trip checklist fills in at home. Daily logs fill in each evening on the road. The after-the-trip section gets completed at least 48 hours after you return, not before.

Section 01

Before You Go

Complete the pre-trip checklist at least a week before you fly. The more you do here, the less you have to scramble for once you arrive.

  • Trip dates locked, four days minimum. Five or six is ideal.
  • Visiting in the hardest season for you, not the prettiest. Florida in September. Phoenix in July. Boston in February.
  • Two or three candidate neighborhoods identified.
    1.
    2.
    3.
  • Trip priced three ways: warehouse-club, package site, individual components. Costco, Sam's Club, or AAA first if you're a member, then Expedia or Travelocity, then individual on Booking.com.
  • Rental car reserved at the arrival airport. Unless the city is truly walkable (Charleston, St. Pete, Delray Beach).
  • Nearest major hospital identified.
    Hospital
  • At least one Medicare-accepting primary care doctor's phone number on hand. Call from the trip to confirm they're taking new patients.
    Practice / phone
  • Cultural anchor identified (museum, garden, historic district).
    Anchor
  • Travel insurance priced if applicable. Worth a quote if visiting a hurricane zone, traveling June through November, or with pre-existing conditions.
  • The top two questions I want this trip to answer.

Section 03

After the Trip

Wait at least 48 hours after returning home before filling any of this out. No earlier. The scouting trip high needs to wear off before you can read your real reactions honestly.

The "what I didn't like" exercise

Sit down with whoever you are making this decision with and answer one question only: what did you not like about the city? Not what you liked. Only the negatives. They will tell you more than the positives will.
