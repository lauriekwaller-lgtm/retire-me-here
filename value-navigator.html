How we evaluate budget and value

Each city is assigned a budget range based on what a comfortable retirement realistically costs — housing, healthcare, utilities, food, and lifestyle. We then score tax friendliness separately, because a city can have low housing costs but punishing taxes on your retirement income, or vice versa.

The real insight is the combination: a city with a budget score of 9 and a tax score of 10 is a fundamentally different retirement proposition than a city with the same scores reversed. Both dimensions together tell you what your money actually buys.

Key Takeaways — Before You Scroll

🏆 The Best Pure Value Play: Tennessee Cities

Chattanooga and Johnson City are the strongest pure value cities in the database — Budget Range 1 (under $3,500/month) combined with a tax score of 10 (no Tennessee income tax on any retirement income). You get a low cost of living AND keep every dollar of your Social Security, pension, and IRA.

💡 The "Sweet Spot" Cities

Several cities combine Budget Range 2 ($3,500–5,000/mo) with tax scores of 8–9: Corpus Christi TX, San Antonio TX, Knoxville TN, Spokane WA, Tucson AZ, and Fort Worth TX. These are cities where a moderate retirement income goes a genuinely long way.

🏙️ The Northeast Surprise: Philly and Pittsburgh

Most people assume Northeast automatically means expensive. Philadelphia and Pittsburgh break that assumption completely. Philadelphia's median home is $270K — comparable to San Antonio TX — with cost of living only 4–10% above national average. Pittsburgh's median home is $265K, the lowest of any major city in the database. Both cities sit in Budget Range 2. Both have world-class healthcare scoring 10/10. Pennsylvania's flat 3.07% income tax is the lowest flat rate nationally, and Social Security is exempt.

⚠️ The California and New York Premium

Carlsbad, Santa Barbara, Palm Springs, and Saratoga Springs sit in Budget Range 4–5 with tax scores of 2 — the worst combination in the database. California's income tax reaches 13.3% on retirement income. New York tops 10.9%. You're paying premium housing prices AND handing a significant portion of your retirement income to the state.

🔍 Hidden Value: The "Afford More Than You Think" Cities

Fayetteville AR (Budget Range 1, tax score 7), Greenville SC (Range 1, tax score 6), and St. Paul MN (Range 2, tax score 3) all offer surprisingly low housing costs. St. Paul's strong value on housing is offset by Minnesota's aggressive income tax — but Fayetteville and Greenville deliver genuine budget-friendly options that most lists overlook.

🏔️ The Mountain Premium

The most scenic mountain cities carry steep price tags. Park City (Budget Range 5, $1.5M+ median home), Jackson Hole (Range 5, $1.85M median), Sedona (Range 4, $930K median), and Bozeman (Range 4, $700K+) are gorgeous but financially demanding. Durango CO and Missoula MT are the most accessible mountain options.
