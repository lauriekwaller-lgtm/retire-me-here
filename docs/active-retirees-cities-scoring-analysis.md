# RetireMeHere — Top Cities for Active Retirees (Scoring Analysis)

**Purpose:** Identify the strongest fitness-culture cities from the 100-city RetireMeHere database, using a transparent and defensible methodology. This document is the source-of-truth scoring rubric and rationale for the Active Retirees landing page.

**Status:** Final rubric established May 1, 2026. Verified scoring complete for top 25 candidate cities.

---

## Framing (applies to ALL landing pages)

> *"This ranking is drawn from RetireMeHere's curated database of U.S. retirement cities — a sampling of viable retirement destinations, not an exhaustive top-100. We add new cities regularly; if your favorite isn't here yet, it may be in a future update."*

## What this list is measuring

The Active Retirees page is built around a specific question: *"Can a retiree who wants to stay seriously fit maintain that lifestyle here?"*

That means more than "the city has gyms." It means:

- **Weight training access** — gyms with proper barbells, racks, and a visible strength culture (not just treadmill rooms)
- **Modality diversity** — Pilates studios, yoga studios, barre, Lagree, and similar specialty studios
- **Senior-aware programming** — instructors and gyms that explicitly support 55+ athletes (CrossFit Masters classes, "Strength for Longevity" programming, etc.)
- **Year-round usability** — not seasonal resort towns
- **Density** — multiple options so users have real choice, not just one option per category

**What this list deliberately does NOT credit:**
- Pure outdoor recreation (hiking, biking) — that's the Hikers list
- Pickleball/golf-only fitness — that's resort-leisure, not fitness culture
- Pretty walking paths or "active lifestyle" marketing without infrastructure
- Tourism vibe absent built infrastructure

## Scoring Methodology — Fitness Index (FI)

Two weighted categories, each scored 1–10, then composited:

| Category | Weight | What it measures |
|---|---|---|
| **Cat A — Strength Training Access** | 60% | Gym density, presence of serious strength facilities (barbell/powerlifting/CrossFit), visible strength culture, presence of national chains signaling market depth (Lifetime, Equinox), university rec center access where applicable |
| **Cat B — Fitness Modality Diversity** | 40% | Density and quality of Pilates studios (including reformer/Megaformer/Lagree), yoga studios, barre, specialty modalities (HOTWORX, Pure Barre, Club Pilates, BODYBAR, etc.) |

**Formula:** `FI = (Cat A × 0.6) + (Cat B × 0.4)`

### Cat A scoring rubric

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 9–10 | Fitness mecca. Multiple serious strength gyms, visible masters/55+ programming, national chains present, athletes train here. Boulder is the archetype. |
| 7–8 | Strong. Multiple options including at least one serious strength facility. National chain (Lifetime/Equinox) typically present. Real strength culture detectable. |
| 5–6 | Adequate. Mainstream chains (LA Fitness, Anytime) plus a couple of independents. No standout strength culture. |
| 3–4 | Below average. Limited options, no serious facility, weak fitness culture. |
| 1–2 | Poor. Essentially no infrastructure beyond hotel-style gyms or YMCA basics. |

### Cat B scoring rubric

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 9–10 | Exceptional density. Multiple Pilates studios including reformer, multiple yoga studios, specialty modalities (Lagree, barre, HOTWORX) all present, often multiple locations of national chains. Carlsbad is the archetype. |
| 7–8 | Strong. At least one solid option in each major modality (Pilates, yoga, barre), multiple studios of at least one type, national chain presence. |
| 5–6 | Adequate. Some studios in 1–2 modalities, limited diversity. |
| 3–4 | Sparse. Maybe one yoga studio, no Pilates reformer access, no barre. |
| 1–2 | Almost nothing in this category. |

## Key sources & verification methods

- **Google Maps density checks** — for gym/studio counts within 15 miles of city center
- **Direct web searches** — for "Lifetime [city]", "powerlifting [city]", "CrossFit Masters [city]"
- **Studio websites** — verify modality offerings (reformer Pilates, Lagree, barre method)
- **Senior programming signals** — explicit 55+/Masters class language on gym websites
- **National chain presence** — Lifetime Fitness, Equinox, Pure Barre, Club Pilates, etc. as proxies for market depth

---

## Why this framework is defensible

- **Honors what real "active retirees" actually want** — strength training to maintain bone density and muscle mass, not generic "exercise"
- **Distinguishes fitness culture from outdoor recreation** — Vail and Naples have lovely settings but rank low here, correctly
- **Surfaces unexpected winners** — Indianapolis, Columbus, St. Louis (Big Ten + Lifetime culture) score higher than Asheville or Sarasota, which is honest
- **Rewards density over vibe** — a city with 3 Pilates studios beats a city with one "wellness boutique"
- **Detects senior-specific programming** — gyms with 55+ classes signal real awareness of the active retiree segment

## Key patterns from the verification process

**1. The "outdoor-active mid-size town" pattern is consistent.**
Asheville (5.4 → 7.4), Bend (7.0 → 8.0), Chattanooga (7.0 → 7.6), Portland ME (7.0 → 7.6), Greenville SC (7.0 → 7.4) — every one moved up under verification. Tourism-vibe assumption underestimates real fitness infrastructure.

**2. Big Ten / sports-anchored metros are stronger than expected.**
Indianapolis (7.0 → 8.0), Columbus (7.0 → 8.0). Big Ten college sports culture combined with Lifetime Fitness presence creates Tier 1 fitness ecosystems.

**3. The aging-athlete signal is real and detectable.**
Multiple verified cities have explicit senior strength programming:
- Whitebelt Athletics (Chattanooga) — ForeverFit 55+ class
- Beacon Community Fitness (Portland ME) — "all seasons of life... stay active for the long run"
- StrengthX (Asheville) — bone density/posture for seniors
- Fluid Strength (Bend) — "Strength for Longevity"
- CrossFit Indianapolis — Masters 55+ class
- YMCA Chattanooga — CrossFit Legends 50+

This is valuable signal for the Active Retirees framing.

**4. Resort/leisure towns consistently fall short.**
Vail, Naples, Park City — all confirmed as not fitness-cultural despite high D8 (Active Wellness) scores in the existing data. The D8 dimension as originally scored was too generous toward outdoor/leisure assets.

## Outstanding uncertainties (honest flags)

**Estimated full scores (3 cities):** Pittsburgh, Eugene, St. Paul are at 7.0 with both categories estimated. They're in the "honorable mention" range so accuracy matters less.
