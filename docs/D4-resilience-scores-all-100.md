## Score bands used

- **9** minimal exposure, stable/affordable insurance — Great Lakes & low-risk inland.
- **7–8** low exposure; insurance normal.
- **5–6** one notable peril, rising premiums, or chronic heat/water/wildfire-smoke stress.
- **3–4** major peril and/or strained insurance market.
- **1–2** compounding catastrophic perils and/or insurance crisis.

## Notes & judgment calls

- **Extreme-heat deserts held a notch above surge coasts** (per your call): Phoenix-style heat/water cities (Scottsdale, Tucson, Las Vegas, St. George) sit at 4–5, above the surge-and-insurance-crisis coasts (FL/LA) at 1–2.

- **Asheville scored 5, not 9** — the post-Helene downgrade is deliberate and is the clearest illustration of why this dimension exists.

- **Same-name cities differ correctly:** Wilmington, DE (7, inland) vs Wilmington, NC (2, hurricane corridor).

- **Seismic/river risks captured** where they dominate: Memphis (4, New Madrid + Mississippi), Salt Lake City (6, Wasatch).
