## What this list is measuring

The Healthcare page is built around a specific question: *"Where can a retiree feel very confident getting required healthcare across many domains?"*

That framing matters. Healthcare for retirees isn't about one famous hospital — it's about depth. As medical needs become more complex with age, retirees benefit from cities where multiple specialties are available locally, where new-patient access exists in reasonable timeframes, and where the local system has demonstrated competence year after year, not just one-time recognition.

**What "confidence across many domains" means in practice:**
- A nationally-ranked or academically-anchored hospital nearby
- Multiple specialties available locally (cardiology, oncology, orthopedics, neurology, etc.)
- Diverse hospital options so wait times and second opinions are real possibilities
- Geriatric-aware care infrastructure where it exists
- Real research and teaching activity (signals that the local system stays current)

---

## Scoring Methodology — Healthcare Confidence Index

Three weighted dimensions, scored 0–10:

| Dimension | Weight | What it measures |
|---|---|---|
| **Hospital Quality** | 40% | Top hospital nationally ranked (US News Honor Roll or Best Hospitals lists). Multi-specialty academic medical center presence. Number of nationally-ranked specialties. Leapfrog grades. |
| **Specialist Access** | 30% | Concentration of cardiologists, oncologists, orthopedists, neurologists, and other geriatric-relevant specialists. Diversity of subspecialties available locally vs. requiring travel. Hospital network depth offering second-opinion paths within the city. |
| **Geriatric Care Depth** | 30% | Presence of dedicated geriatric medicine programs. Memory/dementia care infrastructure. Age-related specialty volume (joint replacement, fall prevention, cardiac rehabilitation). Cancer center presence with cancer types relevant to older adults. Medicare-friendly practice density. |

**Why this framework is defensible:**
- Rewards systems retirees can rely on for decades, not one-time prestige
- "Confidence across many domains" is the actual retirement question — single-specialty excellence (a great heart hospital alone) doesn't help if you also need orthopedic, oncologic, or neurologic care
- Specialist access matters more than aggregate "best hospital" rankings because access — wait times, network options, second opinions — is what shapes real care experience
- Geriatric care depth honors the page's audience: people over 60 thinking about where they'll grow old

**Retiree-specific calibration:**
- A regional flagship hospital with strong Medicare patient experience beats a famous national hospital that doesn't accept Medicare
- Hospital systems with multiple campuses (in-network second opinion possible) outscore single-hospital cities
- Cities with named geriatrics programs (Ochsner geriatrics in nation's top 10%, etc.) score higher
- Cities with strategic proximity to a top hospital deserve their own tier — many retirees genuinely use this strategy

**Big-city exclusion (NYC, Boston, Chicago, LA, Washington DC, San Francisco):**
These cities have world-leading healthcare (Mass General, Cleveland Clinic, etc.) but they're not in the RetireMeHere database because they're not where most people consider retiring. Alexandria captures DC academic medical center access for retirees who want world-class hospitals without DC home prices.

---

## Page Structure — Three Confidence Tiers

Healthcare uses a three-tier structure based on the kind of access available, not on quality gradations between essentially excellent cities:

- **Tier 1 — Anchored by a nationally-ranked top-tier hospital.** The hospital is within the city or about 30 minutes away. Many appear on US News Honor Rolls.
- **Tier 2 — Strong regional healthcare hubs.** Cities anchored by a major university medical center or state flagship hospital. Excellent specialty care without the national-marquee anchor.
- **Tier 3 — Strategic proximity to a top hospital.** Cities that don't have a destination-tier hospital within their limits but where one is within an easy drive. A practical retirement strategy for cost-conscious retirees.

This structure honors three genuinely different retirement strategies: live next to a top hospital, live in a strong regional center, or live where you can drive to a top hospital when needed.
